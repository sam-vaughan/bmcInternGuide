Overview

  • If you have questions, call Radiology resident to clarify optimal type of study
  • For STAT CXR, ask unit coordinator to call radiology
  • Where to find forms:
    Internal.bmc.org->Dept->Radiology->Forms

CT

  • Fill out Contrast history form if needed and place in front of chart
  • head CT w/o contrast before LP to r/o high ICP
  • IV contrast: tumor, source of inflammation/infection
  • PO contrast: when checking for pathology of GI- ie: obstruction, diverticular dx
  • *** If AKI or poor renal function (eGFR <60) pt may need pre-hydration before IV contrast: IV NS or isotonic NaHCO3 (150mL in 850mL of H20) @ 3ml/kg 1 hour before, 1ml/kg 6 hours after +/- acetylcysteine

*** Klima T. Sodium chloride vs. sodium bicarbonate for the prevention of contrast medium-induced nephropathy: a randomized controlled trial. Eur Heart J. 2012;33 (16):2071.

MRI

  • Fill out MRI history AND contrast form and place in front of chart
  • If patient has hardware, find out what type (serial number, etc) and see if clear for study

Nuclear Medicine

  • clarify if patient needs to be NPO at MN and if any medications needs to be held in the AM (ie; stress test- hold beta blockers)

IR

  • Fill out form IR consult form (**ensure you have recent INR, BMP, CBC) and fax to 41698
  • All patients must be NPO 6 hours before procedure
  • If you have any questions page the IR resident: p. 4763
